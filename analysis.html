OLD REGIME (Feb–Mar 2025): 14.9 kWh/day Heater: 9.6 kWh/day • Element run-time: 3.1 h/day Household: 5.2 kWh/day (low — early days after move-in) SUMMER (Jul–Aug 2025): 14.6 kWh/day Heater: 8.4 kWh/day • Element run-time: 2.7 h/day Household: 6.2 kWh/day (settled baseline) NEW REGIME (Dec 2025–Jan 2026): 26.1 kWh/day Heater: 18.5 kWh/day • Element run-time: 5.9 h/day Household: 7.6 kWh/day (gradual increase from more devices) THE GAP: +11.2 kWh/day Heater increase: +8.9 kWh/day (79% of gap) ← TIMER REGIME CHANGE Household increase: +2.4 kWh/day (21% of gap) ← MORE DEVICES OVER TIME WHY DOES THE SAME HEATER USE 2x THE ENERGY? Old regime: element enabled ~6 h/day (in cheap-rate windows) New regime: element enabled ~20 h/day (24/7 minus 16–20) Old regime: tank held at thermostat setpoint only during windows. Between windows, tank cooled freely. No energy consumed. Average tank temperature across the day: well BELOW setpoint. Lower avg temp → lower heat loss rate → less energy needed. New regime: tank held at FULL setpoint ~20 hours/day. Every temperature dip immediately corrected. Average tank temperature across the day: AT or NEAR setpoint. Higher avg temp → higher heat loss rate → more energy needed. The old enforced off-periods acted as free temperature setback. The new regime eliminates that setback = more standing losses replenished. ELEMENT: 3.11 kW (measured). Identical in both regimes. It's not a different element. Not a changed thermostat. Not more hot water usage. It's purely more run-time: 3.1 h/day → 5.9 h/day.
